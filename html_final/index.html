A Fractured Market

In 2019, the gap between youth and established workers was manageable. Everyone played by the same rules.

The Great Divergence

Then came the split. While automation and the pandemic barely scratched established careers, youth unemployment skyrocketed, exposing the jobs most vulnerable to AI.

Left Behind

Today, nearly 1 in 4 young people cannot find work. David’s layoff thrusts him from the safety of the blue line directly into this hostile red reality.

As a matter of fact, lots of jobs have been replaced by AI

While David's story is dramatic, it's just one of many. In Durham Region, entire occupations have already been automated away, leaving workers scrambling to retrain.

Marcus's Dilemma

Marcus graduated from his undergraduate program only to discover his major— computer programming—has been rendered half-obsolete by AI. Now he's forced into a Master's program just to stay competitive.

The Automation Wave

These aren't new jobs disappearing slowly. They're vanishing fast. The occupations most exposed to AI replacement are those requiring routine, codifiable skills— exactly the entry-level work new graduates depend on.

But every revolution creates its own stars.

With the disruption of AI, a new galaxy of opportunities is born.
